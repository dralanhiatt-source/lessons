LESSONS
THAT
LAST
What I wish someone had told me sooner.
01
Lesson One
BE
ON
TIME
Showing up on time is showing respect. For others. For yourself. For the opportunity in front of you. It costs nothing and says everything.
02
Lesson Two
PAY IT
FORWARD
Do something kind for someone — not because they asked, not because you'll be repaid. Your brain releases serotonin and dopamine. You feel better. They feel better. The world shifts, just slightly, in the right direction.
03
Lesson Three
BE
CONFIDENT
Look the part. Approachable. Attentive. Caring. Kind. Smiling. These aren't just personality traits — they are skills you can practice until they become who you are.
04
Lesson Four
CHOOSE
YOUR
CIRCLE
Find five people who are already living the life you want to build. Spend time with them. Their habits, their mindset, their standards — they become yours. You are the average of the people closest to you.
05
Lesson Five
DREAM BIG
THEN DREAM
BIGGER
Whatever your ceiling is — raise it. The dream you're afraid to say out loud? That's the one worth chasing. See yourself there. Feel what it feels like. Then get to work.
06
Lesson Six
TIME +
DEDICATION
Real flashcards, not digital. Write your notes by hand. Draw diagrams. Label things. Your brain encodes differently when your hand moves. Grit, repetition, self-control — these are not born, they are built.
07
Lesson Seven
GO ON
ADVENTURES
Go to another country for college — Germany is free. Volunteer on a Mercy Ship in Africa. Go on a mission trip. Find unusual ways to accomplish ordinary goals. The person who takes the road less traveled always has a better story.
08
Lesson Eight
SIT BY
A LAKE.
BREATHE.
When the world accelerates, you slow down. Meditate. Use stillness as a weapon against stress. See yourself achieving your biggest dreams with total clarity — your mind cannot distinguish between what you vividly imagine and what is real.
09
Lesson Nine
PUT DOWN
THE PHONE
Be in the moment with the people you love. Cultivate real relationships — not followers, not likes, not reactions. Look someone in the eye. Ask how they really are. Be the kind of person people feel better after talking to.
10
Lesson Ten
USE AI.
IT'S THE
FUTURE.
Technology is a tool. Use it for your good — not to replace your thinking, but to amplify it. The students who learn to work with AI now will have an enormous advantage over those who wait. This is not coming. It is already here.
11
Lesson Eleven
PERSEVERE
THROUGH
FAILURE
Previous failures, closed minds, fear to fail — these are not stops. They are teachers. Bias and comfort zones will shrink your world if you let them. Stay flexible. Stay adaptable. Try new things even when you might be wrong.
12
Lesson Twelve
NEVER
STOP
LEARNING
Learn about everything. History, science, art, business, medicine, philosophy, languages. The most interesting people in every room are the ones who are genuinely curious about everything. Become that person.
13
Lesson Thirteen
READ THE
ROOM
Social cues. Body language. Read what isn't being said. A good eye contact, an open posture, a genuine smile — these communicate more than your words. Become someone who makes others feel seen.
14
Lesson Fourteen
YOUR
ATTITUDE
IS A CHOICE
You will not always control what happens to you. You will always control how you respond. A positive attitude in difficult circumstances is not naivety — it is the most powerful form of self-mastery there is.
From Dr. Alan Hiatt
